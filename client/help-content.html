Overview

Geometry Explorer is one interactive view: pick whether you are working with flat figures or solids, choose a figure, drag the sliders (or type values), and watch the preview and measurements update together.

Getting started

  1. Choose Flat or solid: 2D shapes show perimeter and area; 3D solids show surface area and volume.
  2. Pick a figure from the list.
  3. Use the sliders next to each dimension to change size.
  4. Read Measurements and compare them to the canvas preview.

2D vs 3D

For flat shapes, perimeter is the distance around the figure (for a circle, that length is called circumference). Area measures how much surface the shape covers inside its boundary, in square units (u²).

For solid figures, use surface area (total area of the outer surface) and volume (space inside the solid), in u² and u³ respectively.

FAQ

What does “u”, “u²”, and “u³” mean?

They are abstract unit labels so you can focus on the relationships. You can think of u as any length unit (meters, inches, and so on); u² and u³ are the matching square and cube units.

Why does the circle list “circumference” instead of “perimeter”?

They are the same idea: the distance around the circle. The word circumference is the usual name for a circle’s perimeter.

How do I close this help panel?

Press Escape, click outside the dialog, or use the close control in the modal header.
